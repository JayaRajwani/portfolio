● LIVE DEMO case_01

Vantage: AI Compliance Engine

A deterministic pipeline that catches regulatory violations and cites the exact rule, with zero hallucinated citations.

Problem

Marketing compliance review for regulated industries is slow and inconsistent. Every claim in every asset has to be checked against FINRA, FTC, and SEC rules, and generic LLM review cannot be trusted when it recalls rule text from memory instead of the actual policy.

Architecture

A five-stage deterministic pipeline: layout aware document parsing, spatial proximity mapping between claims and disclaimers, claim extraction, grounded rule retrieval, and a constrained LLM verdict step that can only cite rules it was explicitly given.

Key decisions

Retrieval happens per chunk rather than loading the full policy corpus into context. That keeps the signal high and prevents missed violations. The no hallucinated citations guarantee is enforced structurally, not just requested in a prompt.

Stack

OCR and layout detection, hybrid vector and knowledge graph retrieval, LLM judge verdicts, structured JSON contracts.

Outcome

This architecture pattern was applied to a defense-client compliance product at Traversaal and reduced compliance review timelines by 30%. The public build analyzes a full marketing asset in ~30 seconds.

● PRODUCTION case_02

Enterprise Retail Client: Multi Agent Product Search & Quoting

Specialized agents for search, comparison, bundling, and quoting, coordinated through a shared protocol instead of one giant prompt.

Problem

An enterprise retail client's customer facing teams needed to search a large, fragmented product catalog, compare vendors, evaluate bundles, and generate mixed quotes. The workflow was too broad and stateful for a single agent chatbot to handle reliably.

Architecture

A multi agent system on Vertex AI, Google ADK, and Gemini, using MCP for tool access and A2A for agent coordination. Separate agents own search, vendor comparison, bundle evaluation, and quote generation; each communicates through a shared protocol rather than sharing one large context window.

Key decisions

Splitting responsibilities into specialized agents kept each agent's context small enough to evaluate independently. That mattered for a client facing system that had to clear evaluation gates before launch. A2A also let each agent be built and tested in isolation before integration.

Stack

Vertex AI, Google ADK, Gemini, MCP, A2A.

Outcome

Enabled product search, vendor comparison, bundle evaluation, and mixed quote generation in a single client facing flow.

● LIVE case_03

Transconomy Roadmap: AI Powered Roadway Asset Extraction

Turns raw roadway imagery into standards aligned asset inventories across 300+ asset types, without a manual review pass.

Problem

Transportation agencies needed to convert raw geotagged roadway imagery into standardized, auditable asset inventories, a process that traditionally took months of manual review.

Architecture

A computer vision pipeline using YOLO, ResNet, and Mask2Former detects and classifies 300+ roadway asset types, including signs, striping, sidewalks, and guardrails, directly against MUTCD and MIRE industry standards. A human review layer flags duplicates and anomalies for review. The pipeline runs on AWS, Spark, and Airflow to process large near real time batches of imagery.

Key decisions

Classifying against MUTCD/MIRE standards from the start (rather than generic object detection) meant outputs were immediately usable in agencies' compliance and reporting workflows, not just a list of bounding boxes someone still had to translate.

Stack

YOLO, ResNet, Mask2Former, AWS, Spark, Airflow.

Outcome

20% improvement in operational efficiency; scaled to 500K+ geotagged images processed; cut compute costs by 40%.

● LIVE case_05

FHWA InfoTechnology Assistant: Conversational Access to Federal Standards

Lets transportation engineers ask a plain language question and get a cited answer, instead of manually navigating a federal classification system.

Problem

Federal and state transportation engineers had to manually work through dense, tabular nondestructive evaluation documentation to identify the right inspection technology for bridges, pavements, tunnels, or utilities.

Architecture

A knowledge graph backed retrieval assistant scoped only to curated FHWA source documents, with no open web retrieval. It interprets tables and images alongside text, and grounds every answer in the agency's own reference material.

Key decisions

Restricting the retrieval corpus to FHWA-approved sources was a deliberate trust decision for a federal audience. The system prioritizes "can I show my work" over broader coverage. It gives a quick, direct answer first, with supporting documentation available on follow-up.

Stack

Knowledge graph backed RAG, OCR, LangGraph, FastAPI, AWS Bedrock, OpenSearch, Mistral OCR.

Outcome

Across the broader hybrid RAG work built for FHWA, AASHTO, ASHRAE, and UN-affiliated clients, manual search time was reduced by 80% across a corpus of 10,000+ documents.

● INTERNAL case_06

Motive: Production Reliability for Safety Critical Driver AI

Moved from aggregate accuracy to slice based, true recall evaluation and cut diagnosis time from 3 days to 2 hours across 12 production models.

Problem

A fleet of 12 production driver safety AI models needed continuous reliability oversight. Aggregate accuracy metrics were masking failures concentrated in specific operational segments, which meant problems were often caught late.

Architecture

A slice based evaluation pipeline segmenting model performance across 6 distinct operational contexts, paired with centralized health monitoring dashboards and rollout validation gates that a model has to clear before promotion to production.

Key decisions

Prioritized true recall analysis over aggregate accuracy because a model can look healthy overall while failing badly in the segment that matters most. Built the diagnosis pipeline to be self serve for the team, which is what actually drove the speedup.

Stack

Custom evaluation and monitoring pipelines, slice based analysis, precision and recall tracking dashboards.

Outcome

Diagnosis time cut from 3 days to 2 hours; model failure escalations down 30%; precision and recall improved 5% across 12 active models.

● LIVE case_04

Transconomy Lens: Conversational AI for Transportation Asset Data

Turns roadway asset data into instant answers with natural language querying, returning text, maps, and charts without requiring technical skills.

Problem

Transportation agencies already had rich geospatial asset data from Transconomy Roadmap, but decision makers without GIS or query skills could not get to insight without a technical operator in the loop. Questions like "how many miles of line striping does the city have?" or "which intersections are missing crosswalks?" required someone else to run.

Architecture

A natural language interface layered on top of the Transconomy Roadmap GIS platform, delivered as a premium extension so it inherits the existing asset ontology of 300+ MUTCD and MIRE classified asset types rather than building a new data model. Plain English questions route to a query generation stage against the geospatial asset database; results render as text summaries, interactive GIS maps, or charts depending on the question shape, with exportable reports for downstream sharing.

Key decisions

Every answer surfaces the underlying asset view and a shareable map or report, not just a chat reply. For agency users whose outputs get reused in stakeholder reports and compliance workflows, "show your work" mattered more than conversational polish. Lens also had to plug into existing Transconomy workflows and export directly into the tools teams already use.

Stack

LLM driven natural language querying over GIS and asset databases, interactive map rendering, chart generation, and downloadable reports.

Outcome

Turns questions like "where are stop signs rated in poor condition?" or "which intersections are missing crosswalks?" into maintenance hotspot maps and cost estimates in seconds. Non technical decision makers can use the system directly, without waiting on GIS staff.

● LIVE case_07

Farmdar: CropScan Crop Intelligence

Satellite crop detection paired with a digitized farmer registry, turning cropland detection into farmer, variety, and harvest readiness intelligence.

Problem

Farmdar's satellite based crop monitoring needed to translate into decisions mills and farmers could actually act on. That meant tracking acreage, variety, and harvest readiness across huge, fragmented smallholder farming networks where most records were still kept manually.

Architecture

Remote sensing and computer vision models (ResUNet, XGBoost, LightGBM) identify crop variety and growth stage from satellite imagery. This is paired with a digitized farmer registry so every field maps to a tracked ID, and AWS + Mapbox dashboards turn raw detections into operational maps agencies and mills can use directly.

Key decisions

Prioritized variety level detection over generic crop detection because the real business need was tracking adoption of disease resistant seed varieties. That required knowing what was planted, not just that something was.

Stack

ResUNet, XGBoost, LightGBM, AWS (S3, Lambda, Glue, Airflow), Mapbox.

Outcome

Digitized 10M+ acres of land and onboarded 50,000 farmers; drove 200% revenue growth for the platform. In one deployment for one of Pakistan's largest sugar mills, digitized 4,857 farmers for precise supply chain tracking.
