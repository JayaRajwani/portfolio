Selected case studies

● LIVE DEMO case_01

Vantage — AI Compliance Engine

A deterministic pipeline that catches regulatory violations and cites the exact rule — with zero hallucinated citations.

Problem

Marketing compliance review for regulated industries (finance, fintech) is slow and inconsistent — every claim in every asset has to be manually checked against FINRA, FTC, and SEC rules, and generic LLM review can't be trusted because it recalls rule text from memory instead of the actual policy.

Architecture

A five-stage deterministic pipeline: layout-aware document parsing (typed regions — headline, body, disclaimer) → spatial proximity mapping (measuring distance between claims and their disclaimers) → claim extraction → grounded rule retrieval (hybrid vector + knowledge-graph search over a curated policy corpus) → a constrained LLM verdict step that can only cite rules it was explicitly given.

Key decisions

Retrieval happens per-chunk rather than loading the full policy corpus into context — stuffing everything into the prompt dilutes signal and causes missed violations. The "no hallucinated citations" guarantee is enforced structurally (the model is only shown the top-4 relevant rules and validated against that set), not just requested via prompt.

Stack

OCR/layout detection, hybrid vector + knowledge-graph retrieval, LLM-as-judge verdicts, structured JSON contracts.

Outcome

This architecture pattern — applied to a defense-client compliance product at Traversaal — reduced compliance review timelines by 30%. The public build analyzes a full marketing asset in ~30 seconds.

● PRODUCTION case_02

Enterprise Retail Client — Multi-Agent Product Search & Quoting

Specialized agents for search, comparison, bundling, and quoting — coordinating through a shared protocol instead of one giant prompt.

Problem

An enterprise retail client's customer-facing teams needed to search a large, fragmented product catalog, compare vendors, evaluate bundles, and generate mixed quotes — a workflow too broad and stateful for a single-agent chatbot to handle reliably.

Architecture

A multi-agent system on Vertex AI, Google ADK, and Gemini, using MCP for tool access and A2A (agent-to-agent) for coordination. Separate agents own search, vendor comparison, bundle evaluation, and quote generation; each communicates through a shared protocol rather than sharing one large context window.

Key decisions

Splitting responsibilities into specialized agents kept each agent's context small enough to evaluate independently — critical for a client-facing system that had to clear evaluation gates before launch. A2A let each agent be built and tested in isolation before integration, instead of debugging one monolithic prompt.

Stack

Vertex AI, Google ADK, Gemini, MCP, A2A.

Outcome

Enabled product search, vendor comparison, bundle evaluation, and mixed-quote generation in a single client-facing flow.

● LIVE case_03

Transconomy Roadmap — AI-Powered Roadway Asset Extraction

Turns raw roadway imagery into standards-compliant asset inventories — 300+ asset types, no manual review pass required.

Problem

Transportation agencies needed to convert raw geotagged roadway imagery into standardized, audit-ready asset inventories, a process that traditionally took months of manual review.

Architecture

A computer vision pipeline (YOLO, ResNet, Mask2Former) detects and classifies 300+ roadway asset types — signs, striping, sidewalks, guardrails — directly against MUTCD and MIRE industry standards. A human-in-the-loop layer flags duplicates and anomalies for review. The pipeline runs on AWS, Spark, and Airflow to process large real-time batches of imagery.

Key decisions

Classifying against MUTCD/MIRE standards from the start (rather than generic object detection) meant outputs were immediately usable in agencies' compliance and reporting workflows, not just a list of bounding boxes someone still had to translate.

Stack

YOLO, ResNet, Mask2Former, AWS, Spark, Airflow.

Outcome

20% improvement in operational efficiency; scaled to 500K+ geotagged images processed; cut compute costs by 40%.

● LIVE case_04

FHWA InfoTechnology Assistant — Conversational Access to Federal Standards

Lets transportation engineers ask a plain-language question and get a cited answer, instead of manually navigating a federal classification system.

Problem

Federal and state transportation engineers had to manually work through dense, tabular nondestructive-evaluation documentation to identify the right inspection technology for bridges, pavements, tunnels, or utilities.

Architecture

A knowledge-graph-backed, retrieval-augmented conversational assistant scoped only to curated FHWA source documents — no open-web retrieval. It interprets tables and images alongside text, and grounds every answer in the agency's own reference material.

Key decisions

Restricting the retrieval corpus to FHWA-approved sources only was a deliberate trust decision for a federal audience — the system prioritizes "can I show my work" over broader coverage. Designed for progressive disclosure: a quick, direct answer first, with the option to dig into supporting documentation on follow-up.

Stack

Knowledge-graph-backed RAG, OCR, LangGraph, FastAPI, AWS Bedrock, OpenSearch, Mistral OCR.

Outcome

Across the broader hybrid RAG work built for FHWA, AASHTO, ASHRAE, and UN-affiliated clients, manual search time was reduced by 80% across a corpus of 10,000+ documents.

● INTERNAL case_05

Motive — Production Reliability for Safety-Critical Driver AI

Moved from aggregate accuracy to slice-based, true-recall evaluation — cutting diagnosis time from 3 days to 2 hours across 12 production models.

Problem

A fleet of 12 production driver-safety AI models needed continuous reliability oversight. Aggregate accuracy metrics were masking failure that was concentrated in specific operational segments — meaning problems were being caught late, if at all.

Architecture

A slice-based evaluation pipeline segmenting model performance across 6 distinct operational contexts, paired with centralized health-monitoring dashboards and rollout-validation gates that a model has to clear before promotion to production.

Key decisions

Prioritized true-recall analysis over aggregate accuracy — a model can look fine in aggregate while failing badly in one segment that matters most. Built the diagnosis pipeline to be self-serve for the team, rather than requiring manual review for every investigation, which is what actually drove the speed-up.

Stack

Custom evaluation/monitoring pipelines, slice-based analysis, precision/recall tracking dashboards.

Outcome

Diagnosis time cut from 3 days to 2 hours; model failure escalations down 30%; precision and recall improved 5% across 12 active models.

● LIVE case_06

Transconomy Lens — Conversational AI for Transportation Asset Data

Turn your roadway asset data into instant answers with AI-powered natural-language querying — get text, maps, and charts back with no technical skills required.

Problem

Transportation agencies already had rich geospatial asset data from Transconomy Roadmap, but decision-makers without GIS or query skills couldn't get to insight without a technical operator in the loop. Questions like "how many miles of line striping does the city have?" or "which intersections are missing crosswalks?" required someone else to run — the value in the data was gated behind expertise.

Architecture

A natural-language interface layered on top of the Transconomy Roadmap GIS platform, delivered as a premium extension so it inherits the existing asset ontology (300+ MUTCD/MIRE-classified asset types) rather than building a new data model. Plain-English questions route to a query-generation stage against the geospatial asset database; results render as text summaries, interactive GIS maps, or charts depending on the question shape, with exportable reports for downstream sharing.

Key decisions

Every answer surfaces the underlying asset view and a shareable map or report — not just a chat reply. For agency users whose outputs get reused in stakeholder reports and compliance workflows, "show your work" mattered more than conversational polish. Integration also had to be non-disruptive: Lens plugs into existing Transconomy workflows and exports directly into the tools teams already use.

Stack

LLM-driven natural-language querying over GIS/asset database, interactive map rendering, chart generation, downloadable reports.

Outcome

Turns questions like "where are stop signs rated in poor condition?" or "which intersections are missing crosswalks?" into maintenance-hotspot maps and cost estimates in seconds — usable by non-technical decision-makers directly, not just GIS staff.

● LIVE case_07

Farmdar — CropScan Crop Intelligence

Satellite crop detection paired with a digitized farmer registry — turning "is this cropland" into "which farmer, which variety, ready when."

Problem

Farmdar's satellite-based crop monitoring needed to translate into decisions mills and farmers could actually act on — tracking acreage, variety, and harvest readiness across huge, fragmented smallholder farming networks where most records were still kept manually.

Architecture

Remote-sensing and computer vision models (ResUNet, XGBoost, LightGBM) identify crop variety and growth stage from satellite imagery. This is paired with a digitized farmer registry so every field maps to a tracked ID, and AWS + Mapbox dashboards turn raw detections into operational maps agencies and mills can use directly.

Key decisions

Prioritized variety-level detection over generic crop/no-crop classification, because the real business need — tracking adoption of disease-resistant seed varieties — required knowing what was planted, not just that something was.

Stack

ResUNet, XGBoost, LightGBM, AWS (S3, Lambda, Glue, Airflow), Mapbox.

Outcome

Digitized 10M+ acres of land and onboarded 50,000 farmers; drove 200% revenue growth for the platform. In one deployment for one of Pakistan's largest sugar mills, digitized 4,857 farmers for precise supply-chain tracking.
